Department Overview

The Account Management Department is the bridge between Wisevu's internal teams and its clients. Account Managers do not produce SEO deliverables — they own the client relationship, ensure every department delivers on time, consolidate reporting into a single client-facing summary, and are responsible for client satisfaction, retention, and growth. Each AM handles 20–30 clients and communicates with each one through a monthly reporting call.

Core Responsibilities

🚀

Client Onboarding

Setting every new client up for success

  • Own the full onboarding process from contract signed to campaign live
  • Conduct kickoff call with the client — confirm goals, target keywords, service areas, and brand voice
  • Complete and distribute the internal onboarding brief to all four departments
  • Set up client in ClickUp / Asana with correct plan, deliverables, and deadlines
  • Confirm GBP access, website access, and any third-party tool logins are received and shared with the right teams
  • Set expectations: explain what will be delivered each month, how reporting works, and how to reach the AM
  • Target: client fully onboarded and first deliverables in progress within 5 business days of contract signing
📊

Monthly Consolidated Reporting

One clear summary across all departments

  • Collect reports from On-Page SEO, Local SEO / GBP, Content, and Link Building teams by the 25th of each month
  • Review each department's report for accuracy and completeness before consolidating
  • Compile a single client-facing monthly summary — results achieved, work completed, priorities for next month
  • Prepare talking points and key highlights for the monthly reporting call
  • Deliver the report to the client at least 24 hours before the call so they can review it
  • Present the report on the monthly call — lead with wins, address any underperformance transparently
  • Document client feedback and action items from the call and distribute to relevant departments
🔗

Cross-Department Coordination

Keeping all teams aligned on every client

  • Act as the single point of contact between the client and all internal teams
  • Distribute client requests, feedback, and new information to the relevant department immediately
  • Chase departments when deliverables are delayed — never let a missed deadline reach the client without a heads-up
  • Flag priority changes (e.g. client launching a new service, changing location) to all departments at once
  • Run a weekly internal check-in with assigned teams to confirm deliverable status for all clients
  • Ensure no department is working with outdated client information
✅

Deliverable Review & Approval

Quality gate before anything reaches the client

  • Review all client-facing deliverables before they are published or sent — blog posts, service pages, GBP posts, link reports
  • Check for: accuracy, tone consistency with the client's brand, factual correctness, and completeness
  • Return any deliverable that does not meet the standard with specific feedback — not a blanket rejection
  • Approve and log all deliverables in the client's ClickUp / Asana task board
  • Never approve content with medical claims that cannot be verified
  • Target review turnaround: 24 hours for standard deliverables, same day for urgent items
📈

Upselling & Retention

Growing accounts and reducing churn

  • Review every client's plan and results at the 3-month mark — identify gaps in service coverage
  • At 6 months, proactively recommend a plan upgrade if results justify it and the client has room to grow
  • Frame upsells around results and opportunity — never push for revenue's sake
  • Monitor engagement signals: clients who stop responding to calls or emails are churn risks — escalate to team lead
  • Send a proactive check-in email if a client hasn't been contacted in more than 3 weeks outside the reporting cycle
  • Document all upsell conversations and outcomes in the CRM
  • Target: 0 surprise cancellations — every churn risk should be identified and addressed at least 30 days in advance
🚨

Escalation Management

Owning problems before they become crises

  • All client complaints route through the AM first — never let a department respond to a client complaint directly
  • Acknowledge every complaint within 2 hours during business hours
  • Investigate internally before responding — get the facts from the relevant department first
  • Respond to the client with a clear explanation and a concrete resolution plan
  • For serious issues (significant ranking drops, GBP suspension, negative press), escalate to team lead and loop in the relevant department head immediately
  • Document every escalation: what happened, how it was resolved, and what process change prevents recurrence

Monthly Reporting Call Agenda

  • Open with wins — what improved this month and why
  • Walk through organic traffic and ranking trends
  • Cover GBP performance — calls, views, direction requests
  • Summarize content published and links acquired
  • Address any underperformance transparently with context
  • Present the top 3–5 priorities for next month
  • Open for client questions — document all action items
  • Close with a clear summary of what happens next

Call Best Practices

  • Send the report to the client at least 24 hours before the call
  • Keep calls to 30–45 minutes — respect the client's time
  • Never come to a call without knowing the numbers cold
  • If results are down, have a clear explanation and recovery plan ready before the call
  • Record calls with client consent for internal review and training
  • Send a follow-up email within 24 hours summarizing action items
  • Log call notes and outcomes in the CRM immediately after

Upselling & Retention

Retention is the AM's most important metric. A client who stays and grows is worth far more than a new client acquired.

📈 Upsell signals

When to recommend an upgrade

  • Client is ranking on page 2 for high-value keywords — more links or content could push them to page 1
  • Client has service areas not covered by existing location pages
  • Client's GBP competitors have significantly more reviews — review management add-on makes sense
  • Client mentions wanting faster results — discuss what a higher-tier plan delivers
  • Client is launching a new service or location — expansion opportunity
  • 3-month results show momentum — right time to invest more while growth is happening
⚠️ Churn warning signs

When retention is at risk

  • Client stops responding to emails or misses reporting calls
  • Client questions the value of SEO or asks "what am I paying for"
  • Rankings have dropped for 2+ consecutive months without explanation
  • Client mentions a competitor agency reaching out to them
  • Client has had an unresolved complaint for more than 5 business days
  • Client's business is going through financial difficulty or a rebrand

Monthly Workflow

The AM's month runs on a consistent cycle across all 20–30 clients. Standardization is what makes this volume manageable.

1
Deliverable Check-In (Week 1)
Confirm all departments have their briefs and are on track for the month. Flag any clients with upcoming changes (new service, address update, seasonal campaign) and distribute to relevant teams. Review any outstanding action items from last month's client calls.
2
Deliverable Review (Week 2–3)
Review and approve all client-facing content as it comes in from departments — blog posts, service pages, GBP posts. Return anything that doesn't meet standard with specific feedback. Approve and log all completed deliverables in ClickUp / Asana.
3
Collect Department Reports (By 25th)
Chase all four departments for their monthly reports by the 25th. Review each report for accuracy, completeness, and anything that needs to be flagged or explained before it reaches the client. Do not consolidate until all four reports are in and reviewed.
4
Compile & Send Consolidated Report (By 27th)
Build the client-facing monthly summary from all four department reports. Lead with wins, include key metrics, summarize work completed, and outline next month's priorities. Send to the client at least 24 hours before their scheduled reporting call.
5
Monthly Reporting Calls (Week 4)
Conduct monthly reporting calls with all assigned clients. Present results, address questions, and document action items. For any client showing churn signals, schedule a follow-up within the week. Log all call notes in the CRM same day.
6
Follow-Ups & Next Month Setup (End of Month)
Send post-call follow-up emails within 24 hours summarizing action items. Distribute any new client instructions or changes to departments for the coming month. Review client health across all accounts — flag any retention risks to the team lead before the new month begins.
